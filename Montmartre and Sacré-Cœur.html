Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur

Built by Gustave Eiffel's company for the 1889 Paris Exposition (World's Fair) as a part of the celebrations to mark the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. It was initially criticized by some artists and intellectuals, but it became an integral part of the Parisian skyline.

The tower, constructed of wrought iron, stands at approximately 324 meters (1,063 feet) tall. It was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York City in 1930.

Originally planned to stand for 20 years, it was almost dismantled after the Exposition. However, it was retained due to its value for communication purposes as a radio antenna.

The Eiffel Tower became an enduring symbol of France and Paris, representing the city's elegance, engineering, and artistic achievement.
